Our Vision for Missions

Missions, at home and abroad, is a foundational part of who we are as a church. This commitment extends to our 300 plus missionaries and ministries and more than 105 countries through financial support, as well as prayer and personal encouragement. We desire for our heart to match the heartbeat of God for the lost and hurting around the world. The purpose of this “Strategy for World Ministries” is to articulate God’s clear direction regarding missions for our church. We pray this strategy will help steer our future direction and bridge our calling to future church generations.



  • Principle 1: Prayer is Paramount

    The Lord has shown us that prayer is, and must continue to be, the primary gift we give to missions. Prayer connects the hearts of people within the local church to the heart of God and His love for the world. We understand that nothing of eternal value happens without prayer.

    “I believe it will only be know on the Last Day how much has been accomplished in missionary work by the prayers of earnest believers at home.. I do earnestly covet a volume of prayer for myself and the work-but who will give this?” James Fraser

  • Principle 2: Increasingly Affect the World for Jesus Christ

    We will continue monthly support to an ever-increasing staff of missionaries locally and globally. Our first consideration for support will be to missionaries and world missions that are accountable to the Assemblies of God World Ministries, although we will reach out to others as the Lord allows. We will not limit the number of missionaries the Lord would have us support, but we will continue to encourage “new harvesters” with monthly commitments in prayer and finances. This will:

    - Keep our focus on Christ’s teaching to ask the Lord of the Harvest to send forth laborers into His harvest.
    - Keep our congregation from becoming complacent in “our” accomplishments by continually encouraging us to provide for the needs of the world.

  • Principle 3: Build Relationships

    We will continue to partner and build personal relationships from within our missionary staff. We will develop at least one key partnership in North America, Africa, Asia, Europe, Latin America and Oceania. Currently, we have partners in China, Ecuador, Japan, several nations in the middle East and North Africa, India, Moldova, Nepal, Philippines, Senegal, and Uzbekistan to name a few. We will continue to build close relationships with a limited number of our missionaries and their families. This will:

    - Foster long-term relationships and influence in different areas of the world.
    - Influence different areas of the world in a more substantial way.
    - Provide relational benefits for the missionary family and our church family accomplished through ongoing prayer and monthly contact with each of our missionary staff. These partnerships will have a higher priority for funding beyond monthly support. If these servants change locations or area of ministry, we will ask the Lord for His direction in order to continue to impact every continent. These partnerships will not restrict us from helping meet other needs that may arise on a short term or one-time basis.

    Missionaries-in-Residence – As we are able, we will offer housing to specific missionaries while they are itinerating. While they are with us, we will do our best to make them feel like part of the church. We will give the missionaries-in-residence and their family a place to connect and a strong base of support.

  • Principle 4: Show Compassion

    As a church family, we will provide one-time or ongoing support in emergency situations as natural disasters, war, calamities, and other special needs. This will:

    - Allow us to be His instruments of compassion at critical moments in regions of the world that need His touch.
    - Open doors to evangelism through a practical expression of God’s love.

    Ministries that serve as our conduit in critical moments and ongoing areas of need include Convoy of Hope, Disaster Relief, Healthcare Ministries, Mercy Ships, and Project Rescue, just to name a few.

  • Principle 5: Distribute the Word of God & Disciple the Nations

    We will aid in giving the Bible to every human being. Distributing the written Word, whether print or digital, will allow the Gospel to be spread with great speed and even greater results. In addition, we will support Global University and Bible colleges and universities across the globe. We will support the distribution of Bibles, tracts and other literature through the Fire Bible, Light for the Lost, Bible Alliance, the International Bible Society and Boys and Girls Missionary Challenge (BGMC).

  • Principle 6: Reaching the Unreached

    We will be intentional about reaching people in our world who have not heard the message of Jesus. We will focus prayer, attention, and resources to those thousands of tribal and language groups who have not had a witness of Jesus. With God’s help, we will do our part to touch the “unreached” wherever they are and however we can. We will not neglect currently supported places and partnership but will strive to reach the unreached and the never reached, in every way possible.

  • Principle 7: Involve Our Entire Church Body

    We will seek to have EVERYONE who calls Cornerstone their home church participate in World Ministries in some capacity. This will:

    - Help everyone in our body focus on Christ and His great commission.
    - Help individuals and families form long-term personal relationships with missionaries, which will reap great benefits for all.
    - Raise up a new generation of young people who understand missions.

    We will accomplish this through several key initiatives, such as prayer for the lost and for missionaries at all age levels within the church and through personal interaction with missionaries on a regular basis. We will encourage our congregation to pray for them at weekly gatherings and prayer meetings. We will give opportunity for people of all ages to respond to the call to missions by encouraging mission participation through prayer, financially resourcing and going on short-term and long-term mission trips. We will ask God to raise up new harvesters from within Cornerstone Church who will be sent out to serve locally and globally. This will increase missions awareness within the church and keep it at the forefront of what Cornerstone is all about.
