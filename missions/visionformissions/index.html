Our Vision for Missions


"One Church Can Change the World"


Partnering in missions is how we as a local church work to establish the global Church among all people, at home and abroad. We currently partner with 300 plus missionaries and ministries in more than 105 countries and hope to expand our partnership even further as God enables. Our strategy for partnership in missions is guided by 7 principles:



  • Principle 1: We Will Pray

    Prayer is, and must continue to be, the primary gift we give to missions since it connects the church to the heart of God and His love for the world, and nothing of eternal value happens without prayer.

    "Every great movement of God can be traced to a kneeling figure." -D.L. Moody

  • Principle 2: We Will Increasingly Affect the World For Jesus Christ

    We will not limit the number of missionaries the Lord would have us partner with, but we will continue to encourage new workers with monthly commitments in prayer and finances, giving first consideration to Assemblies of God missionaries and ministries, and extending partnership to others as the Lord allows.

    "The mark of a great church is not its seating capacity, but it's sending capacity." -Mike Stachura

  • Principle 3: We Will Build Relationships

    We will develop strategic, long-term, mutually-beneficial relationships among our missionary staff. Our desire is to have at least one key partnership in every region of the world. While these partnerships will have a higher priority for funding beyond monthly support, they will not restrict us from helping meet other needs that may arise on a short term or one-time basis. Additionally, we will also offer housing, a place to serve, and relational support to missionary staff on itineration who we will invite to serve as Missionaries-in-Residence.

    "Missions is not just for missionaries; God's call is for all." -Ann Dunagan

  • Principle 4: We Will Reach the Unreached

    We will intentionally give focused attention, prayer, and resources toward establishing the church among those thousands of tribal and language groups who have not had a witness of the gospel, and among whom the indigenous church either does not yet exist or is not yet mature enough to do the work of mission.

    "Lost people matter to God, and so they must matter to us." -Keith Wright

  • Principle 5: We Will Show Compassion

    Believing that God cares for the whole person - body, mind, and spirit - we will provide support as instruments of His holistic compassion when natural disasters, war, calamities, and other special needs may arise.

    "Do you know that nothing you do in this life will ever matter, unless it is about loving God and loving the people He has made?" -Francis Chan

  • Principle 6: We Will Distribute the Word of God and Disciple the Nations

    Believing that “faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God” (Rom. 10:17), we will aid in presenting the Bible in print, audio, visual and digital forms to every human being.

    “If Jesus Christ be God, and died for me, then no sacrifice can be too great from me to make for Him.” -C. T. Studd

  • Principle 7: We Will Involve Our Entire Church Body

    We want everyone who calls Cornerstone their home church to participate in missions in some way. Church-wide participation in mission increases the focus on Christ and His commission. It facilitates personal relationships with missionaries that yield long-term benefits for all, and aides in raising up a new generation that loves and understands missions. We will give opportunity for people of all ages to respond to the call to missions by encouraging participation in prayer, giving, and serving on mission trips, as well as asking God to raise up new missionaries from within Cornerstone Church to be sent out to serve locally and globally.
