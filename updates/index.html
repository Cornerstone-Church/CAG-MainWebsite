  • June 17th, 2020

    Dear Cornerstone Family,

    Thank you for your perseverance and your heart for God. You have been faithful to love, serve, and touch others during this time of not being able to gather together. You have modeled faithfulness and have pressed in through prayer. Prayer has always been the key to Cornerstone Church. Like never before, prayer is essential.

    Just last week, we received news that some restrictions are lifting in Prince George’s County. This will allow us to meet in person in smaller numbers. We are very excited to announce our first time of gathering together will be this Saturday night, June 20 at 7pm for our “In the Gap” prayer meeting. We invite you to join us in person, but you must register online at cag.org/events. Since we are limited to 150 people this week, please register soon. If you are unable to attend in person, join us online on the Cornerstone Facebook page. Thanks for praying with us.

    Below you will see some of the measures of safety and sanitizing we are taking to provide the safest environment possible.

    Please Attend Online If:
    - You are sick, have a fever, or a documented temperature of 100.4 F or higher, present any signs of cold, flu, or COVID-19 symptoms.
    - You have been in contact with anyone who has had COVID-19 or in proximity with a lot of people, such as protesting, in the past 14 days.
    - If you are in a high-risk group.

    Before You Arrive:
    - You must register to attend and fill out a waiver prior to the service. Go to cag.org/services/.
    - Capacity is limited to 150 people.
    - Before every service, the Sanctuary and all high-touch surfaces will be disinfected.

    When You Arrive:
    - Please arrive by 6:45 and wait in your car until directed to enter.
    - Everyone 2 years old and above must wear a mask to enter and during your entire time in the church building. You will not be allowed in the building without a mask.
    - Your temperature will be checked before entering the building by Servant Ministers who will be properly equipped with PPE and no-touch thermometers.
    - Enter the building at the double doors of the church’s main entrance. Ushers will direct you to your seat. Seating is available on a first come, first served basis, from the front of the Sanctuary to the rear.
    - Our Sanctuary has been set to allow for social distancing. We have measured and observed the federal standards – six feet between family units.
    - If there is a line of people coming in, please stay six feet apart.

    During the Prayer Service:
    - Everyone must remain at their assigned seat until directed to exit.
    - Restrooms will be available on the main level. Only two people at a time are allowed to be in the restrooms.
    - Drinking fountains will not be available for use.

    Exiting the Service:
    - Under the guidance of Ushers, you will be dismissed from service by row, starting at the rear of Sanctuary and moving forward.
    - Upon exiting the Sanctuary, no one is to remain in the Lobby. You must immediately exit the church. Common areas and gathering spaces of the building will be closed. This includes all the first level, Lobby, Café, and any other place where people congregate.

    We look forward to seeing you in person this Saturday! Together for Jesus, Pastor Mark

  • March 14th, 2020

    Dear Cornerstone Family,

    “For He will rescue you from every trap and protect you from deadly disease. He will cover you with His feathers. He will shelter you with His wings. His faithful promises are your armor and protection.” Psalm 91:3-4

    Don’t Be Afraid, Trust God
    “Do not be afraid of the terrors of the night, nor the arrow that flies in the day. Do not dread the disease that stalks in darkness, nor the disaster that strikes at midday.” Verse 5-6

    I am so thankful that in the midst of any storm, our God is a shelter, a refuge, and a place of safety. We can put all of our trust in Him, and depend on Him alone. Fear is not how God made us to live. We are people of faith and trust in our powerful God. My prayer for you is that you and your families will breath in His peace and let your faith in God rise up like never before.

    We are praying for each one of you and are asking God for His protection, His healing, His keeping power and also His presence to overwhelm you. We all are putting our trust in the living God during this time for all that are impacted by COVID-19 and its effects.

    I want to encourage you while many of us are at home or are curtailing other activities to put God first. Let’s pray and see God answer and move in lives and hearts, and in the nations. Most of you have seen the video we released on Thursday, but if you have not, you can view it here.

    What Should We Be Praying For?
    • Pray for healing for those who have the Coronavirus: Our God is the Healer!
    • Pray for protection against the virus: He will protect You!
    • Pray for God to stop the spread of the virus: If His people will pray then God will heal our land!
    • Pray for people to turn to God: Many people are looking now for answers, the only one is Jesus!
    • Pray that God would make us witnesses of His grace: We can be His witnesses of peace and hope!
    • Pray for other brothers and sisters around the World: Many nations are suffering much more than we are.
    • Pray for our missionaries around the nation and the World: Pray that God will protect them, provide for them, and use them.
    • Pray for Cornerstone Church: For all the people who call Cornerstone their home, pray that God will use us to touch others for Jesus during this time and beyond.

    How Should We Take Precaution?
    Your well-being and safety matters to us. With that in mind, we encourage you to take precautions to keep yourself and others safe. You can help stop the spread of the virus in the following ways:
    • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after going to the bathroom, before eating and after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.
    • Cover your sneeze or cough with a tissue or your arm.
    • Avoid touching your face.
    • If you are returning from a high-alert area, we urge you to refrain from attending church events, classes, small groups and services until the 14-day time-frame has been observed.
    • If you or someone you know tests positive for COVID-19, please let us know so we can find a way to help, pray for everyone involved and take any necessary precautions here at Cornerstone Church.
    • Anyone, especially if you have a weak immunity, above the age of 60 should avoid non-essential events.
    • Avoid attending church events, classes, small groups and services if you are feeling ill or have been around someone not feeling well.
    • Practice social distancing. Stay at least 6 feet apart and avoid large crowds.
    Contact the Maryland Department of Health if you have any concerns or questions at https://health.maryland.gov/Pages/contactus.aspx.

    Cornerstone’s Role
    Here are things that we are doing at Cornerstone Church. Our Governor has asked that all gatherings of more than 250 people be stopped for this time, so our staff has met together and have put these things in place.
    • All Church services, events and activities will be canceled, except for prayer services today, March 14 at 3pm, 5pm, and 7pm. The prayer services will be limited to 200 people and in order to attend you will need to sign up at cag.org/events.
    • Our Services will be live streamed at 9am and 11am on Sunday, March 15 with worship and an important message that God has laid on our heart. In order to view the live stream, simply go to our website at cag.org at 9 or 11am. We encourage you to ask others to join us and to ask others to be part of us as well.
    • If you have not yet set up online giving, please do so at cag.org/give! The Ministry of the church here and around the world needs to continue during this time and our missionary partners need our prayer and support. So please be faithful; your giving is important in allowing us to continue to minister effectively.
    • We will continue our Care ministry and reaching out to those in need if the virus affects those in our community. Please email care@cag.org.
    • Helping hands ministry will continue on Tuesday evening for those in need.
    • If you are in need of prayer, please email us at prayer@cag.org.
    • We will be updating you on a regular basis through email and social media.

    Together we are trusting God. As a family, we will see the Lord’s faithfulness to us and through us during this challenging time. None of this has taken God by surprise, so let’s lean on Jesus and see His hand guiding us. Trust Him and believe!

    The Lord says, “I will rescue those who love me. I will protect those who trust in my name. When they call on me, I will answer; I will be with them in trouble. I will rescue and honor them.” Psalm 91:14-15

    Together for Jesus,

    Pastor Mark
