Living Free is a discipleship ministry that uses small group based studies, focused on helping us prevent and overcome roadblocks in our faith. The materials are biblically-based, Christ-centered, and designed to benefit all. Challenging us to examine our own lives and equipping us to reach our community.

For more information on how to get involved contact connections@cag.org.

  • Anger: Our Master or Our Servant

    Anger: Our Master or Our Servant (Creative Use of a Powerful Emotion)

    This 9-session small group study offers a clear explanation of anger, what causes it, and how to recognize when you are sinning with anger. Through these sessions we learn the beginning steps in managing anger, methods to help control anger in our relationships, and ten steps for preventing anger’s control in our life.

    Time: Tuesday | 7:00pm - 8:30pm (1/30 - 3/26)

  • Authentic Christianity

    Authentic Christianity (Authentic Living in an Artificial World)

    This 6-session study discusses how authentic people who are living in authentic Christian Community are powerful witnesses. This study also deals with what authenticity looks like, the value of authenticity and the marks of authentic Christians.

    Time: Monthly - 1st Sundays | 4:00pm in Glen Burnie

  • Facing Fear

    Facing Fear (Anxiety)

    This is a 10-session curriculum designed to help those controlled by anxiety, fear, and worry. It includes guidance in the use of Scripture to build faith, provides biblical coping methods for facing fear, and is a resource of help for anyone ministering to those who are being controlled or held back by anxiety, fear and worry.

    Time: Thursdays | 7:00pm (1/30 - 4/2)

  • Coping with Loss

    Coping with Loss

    Everyone will experience some type of loss during their lifetime. Loss comes in many forms: death, loss of a job, loss of freedom, loss of a relationship and various other types of losses. This 9-session small group study covers a multitude of losses that create pain in a person's life. The course goal common to every lesson is to identify troublesome areas of the losses and disappointments of life and to deal with them in constructive ways.

    Time: Saturdays | 5:30pm (2/1 - 3/28) | Room 110

  • Insight: Godly Character

    Insight: Godly Character (Facilitator Training)

    With its emphasis on developing Christian character, this 9-session group is a powerful tool for those who want to be sure to prevent life-controlling problems from developing in their lives, as well as for those who need to overcome a current problem. This study will enhance people’s understanding of themselves and others.

    Time: Wednesday | 7:30pm (1/29 - 3/25) | Youth Lounge

  • Understanding Depression

    Understanding Depression

    Through this 9-session small group study we get an understanding of things such as: what depression is and how it affects relationships; the relationship between depression and spiritual warfare; ways to fight depression with scripture, prayer and mutual support; the special prayer needs of persons with depression; Bible-based coping skills and how to pray for healing and what to expect.

    Time: Tuesdays | 7:00pm (1/28 - 3/24) | Room 110

  • Committed Couples

    Committed Couples: God's Plan for Marriage & Family

    This 13-session study is designed to help each married couple grow spiritually and emotionally in their relationship to each other through an awareness and application of biblical principles. It is important that each participant enter Committed Couples with the purpose of wanting to be all God would have them to be in their marriage rather than trying to change their partner.

    Time: Thursdays | 7:00pm (1/30 - 4/23) | Room 108

  • A Passionate Pursuit of God

    A Passionate Pursuit of God: Drawing Nearer to Him

    This 7-session small group study shows you how to move into God's inner circle, how to listen for the voice of God, how to enjoy the freedom of worship, and how to embrace the mission of worship.

    Time: Thursdays | 7:00pm (1/30 - 3/12) | Room 111
