Richard (Dick) Frederick Brown
September 7, 1932 - August 15, 2020

Obituary

Richard (Dick) F. Brown of Bowie, MD passed away on August 15, 2020 in a Pasco County, FL hospital of complications following heart surgery, at the age of 87. Born to the late John S. & Annetta M. (Metzger) Brown in Philadelphia, PA on September 7, 1932. Dick graduated from Myerstown High School in 1950 and joined the Navy one month later. During his 3 years of service, he served aboard the USS Mattabesset and the USS Salamonie and was honorably discharged in July 1953.

Dick joined the Air Force in January 1954 and married the lovely Luanne Mae Werner later that year on August 28, 1954. He served in the Air Force for 18 years and was honorably discharged as a Senior Master Sergeant on December 31, 1972. He immediately joined the U.S. Capitol Police on January 3, 1973 and after serving 20 years on the force, he retired yet again, on January 31, 1993. He promptly joined the U.S. Marshal Service as a Deputy U.S. Marshal assigned to the Department of Justice for roughly 12 years before his reassignment to the Department of Homeland Security where he served an additional 2 years before being disabled by a stroke in July, 2007.

While 55 years of service to his country are to be admired and applauded, it was his love of, and dedication to, his Savior, Jesus Christ, and to his family that always came first. Throughout their almost 66 years of marriage, Dick and Luanne raised 10 children and fostered countless infants as they awaited their forever homes.

Cornerstone Assemblies of God Church in Bowie is where Dick and Luanne anchored themselves to God’s call on their lives to actively pursue Christ’s command from Matthew 28:19 “Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit…”. Missions throughout the world, their children, grandchildren and great grandchildren were always at the top of their prayer lives.
